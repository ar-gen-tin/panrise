# Example: Indian Freelancer Setting Up US LLC

**User prompt:** "I'm an Indian software developer doing $60K/year freelancing for US clients. Should I get a Wyoming LLC?"

---

## Panrise Solo Response

### User Profile
`IN | consulting | $60K | US | start_company`

### Recommended Structure
**Wyoming LLC (Single-Member)**

### Why This Works for You
- 0% US federal tax for non-US owners (pass-through, no US-source income)
- Mercury bank account for USD payments from US clients
- Stripe for invoicing — US entity adds credibility with US clients
- Cheapest US LLC to maintain ($60/year state fee)

### Setup Steps
1. Register Wyoming LLC on Secretary of State website — $100 filing fee, 1-3 days
2. Get a registered agent ($50-150/year) — Wyoming Agents ($20/yr cheapest)
3. Apply for EIN — call IRS at +1 267-941-1099 (international line)
4. Open Mercury bank account — remote, ITIN accepted (approval tightened 2025-2026)
5. Connect Stripe — requires company website with service description

### Banking & Payments
- **Primary:** Mercury ($0/month, USD only, FDIC up to $5M)
- **Multi-currency:** Wise Business ($0/month, 50+ currencies)
- **Payments:** Wise invoicing for B2B + Stripe for card payments

### Tax Obligations

**US Side (minimal):**
- Form 5472 + pro-forma 1120: **MANDATORY every year**, even with zero income — $25,000 penalty if missed
- No US income tax (pass-through to India)
- Filing deadline: April 15 (extension to October 15 via Form 7004)

**India Side (important):**
- Declare LLC income in ITR-3, Schedule FA
- LRS remittance: $250,000/year limit via authorized dealer bank
- TCS: 20% on remittances above ₹10 lakh/year (refundable via ITR)
- ODI: Form ODI Part I within 30 days of first capital contribution
- APR (Annual Performance Report): due December 31 each year

### Estimated Costs
| Item | Year 1 | Ongoing |
|------|--------|---------|
| Wyoming filing | $100 | — |
| Registered agent | $50 | $50/year |
| Annual report | $60 | $60/year |
| Form 5472 (CPA) | $500-2,000 | $500-2,000/year |
| **Total** | **$710-2,210** | **$610-2,110/year** |

### Watch Out For
- **Form 5472 is the #1 trap:** Most Indian founders don't know about it until they get a $25K penalty notice
- **Mercury opening is harder now:** Have your website, EIN, and business description ready before applying
- **FEMA compliance:** You must file ODI forms — penalties up to 3x the violation amount
- **Black Money Act:** Failure to disclose LLC in Schedule FA can trigger penalties up to 90% of undisclosed asset value

*This is general guidance. For your specific situation, consult a qualified tax advisor.*
