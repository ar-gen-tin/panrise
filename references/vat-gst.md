# VAT, GST & Sales Tax Compliance for Solo Founders

## Do You Need to Collect Tax from Customers?

It depends on: WHERE your customer is, WHAT you sell, and HOW MUCH you sell.

## EU VAT (Value Added Tax)

### When It Applies
- You sell digital products/services (SaaS, ebooks, courses, software) to EU consumers (B2C)
- You sell to EU consumers regardless of where YOUR company is registered
- Threshold: €10,000/year in total EU B2C sales (then you must register)

### EU VAT Rates (Digital Services)
| Country | Standard Rate | Reduced Digital |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| Germany | 19% | 19% |
| France | 20% | 20% |
| Netherlands | 21% | 21% |
| Spain | 21% | 21% |
| Italy | 22% | 22% |
| Ireland | 23% | 23% |
| Sweden | 25% | 25% |

### VAT OSS (One-Stop Shop)
- Register in ONE EU country, report all EU B2C sales through one portal
- No need to register in each EU country separately
- Available to non-EU businesses via "Non-Union OSS" scheme
- File quarterly returns

### B2B Sales to EU
- If your EU customer provides a valid VAT number → reverse charge (0% VAT, customer self-assesses)
- Always validate EU VAT numbers via VIES system before zero-rating

### Merchant of Record (MoR) — Easiest Solution
Services like **Paddle**, **Lemon Squeezy**, **FastSpring** act as the seller and handle ALL VAT for you:
- They collect VAT from customers
- They file and remit VAT to each country
- You receive net payment
- **Cost**: 5-10% of revenue (includes payment processing)
- **Best for**: Solo founders under €500K revenue who don't want VAT complexity

## US Sales Tax

### When It Applies
- You sell to US customers (digital or physical goods)
- You have "nexus" in a state (physical presence OR economic nexus)
- **Economic nexus**: Most states trigger at $100K in sales OR 200 transactions per year

### Key Facts
- US has NO federal sales tax; it's state-by-state (45 states + DC)
- Digital products/SaaS: Taxability varies by state
  - Taxable in: TX, NY, PA, WA, and ~20 others
  - Not taxable in: CA (SaaS), GA, MO, and others
- **If you have NO US nexus**: You may not need to collect (but rules are evolving)

### Solutions for US Sales Tax
- **Stripe Tax**: Automatic calculation and collection ($0.50/transaction)
- **TaxJar/Avalara**: Full compliance automation
- **Paddle/Lemon Squeezy**: MoR handles it for you

## Australia GST

### When It Applies
- You sell digital services to Australian consumers (B2C)
- Annual revenue from Australian sales > AUD 75,000
- Non-resident suppliers must register for GST

### GST Rate
- 10% on digital supplies
- Must register, collect, and file quarterly BAS (Business Activity Statement)

## Singapore GST

### When It Applies
- Taxable turnover exceeds SGD 1,000,000/year, OR
- You voluntarily register (can be beneficial for input tax claims)
- Non-resident suppliers selling digital services to SG consumers: must register if >SGD 100K

### GST Rate
- 9% (increased from 8% in 2024)

## New Zealand GST

### When It Applies
- Non-resident suppliers of digital services to NZ consumers
- Threshold: NZD 60,000/year in NZ sales

### GST Rate
- 15%

## Japan Consumption Tax (JCT)

### When It Applies
- Foreign businesses selling digital services to Japanese consumers
- Must register for JCT regardless of threshold

### JCT Rate
- 10% (8% reduced rate for some items)

## Canada GST/HST

### When It Applies
- Non-resident suppliers selling digital services to Canadian consumers
- Threshold: CAD 30,000/year in Canadian sales
- Simplified registration available for non-residents

### Rate
- 5% GST (federal) + 0-10% provincial (HST combined up to 15%)

## UK VAT

### When It Applies
- You sell digital services to UK consumers (B2C)
- No threshold for non-UK businesses — must register from first sale
- UK businesses: £90,000 threshold

### UK VAT Rate
- 20% standard rate

## Compliance Strategy for Solo Founders

### Revenue < €10K from any single jurisdiction
→ **Do nothing special** (below most thresholds)
→ Monitor growth to know when you cross thresholds

### Revenue €10K-€100K with global customers
→ **Use a Merchant of Record** (Paddle, Lemon Squeezy)
→ They handle all tax collection and remittance
→ Cost: 5-10% but saves massive compliance headache

### Revenue > €100K with concentrated markets
→ **Register for VAT/GST** in top 2-3 markets
→ Use **Stripe Tax** for automated collection
→ Hire a tax accountant familiar with international digital taxes
→ Cost: $2,000-5,000/year in accounting

### The MoR Decision
| Revenue | Complexity Tolerance | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------------|----------------|
| < $50K | Low | MoR (Paddle/Lemon Squeezy) |
| $50K-$200K | Low | MoR |
| $50K-$200K | High | Self-register top markets + Stripe Tax |
| > $200K | Any | Self-register + accountant + Stripe Tax |

## Key Filing Deadlines

| Jurisdiction | Filing Frequency | Typical Deadline |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| EU VAT OSS | Quarterly | End of month after quarter |
| UK VAT | Quarterly | 1 month + 7 days after period |
| Australia GST | Quarterly | 28 days after quarter |
| Singapore GST | Quarterly | 1 month after quarter |
| US Sales Tax | Varies by state | Monthly/quarterly/annually |

## When Things Go Wrong — Penalties & Remediation

### Late Registration Penalties

Most EU countries impose penalties for selling above the €10,000 threshold without registering. Typical ranges:
- **Minimum penalty**: €500 (small/first-time violation)
- **Maximum penalty**: 5% of unpaid VAT (for sustained non-compliance)
- Interest on unpaid VAT accrues from the date it was due

### Voluntary Disclosure

Most jurisdictions offer reduced penalties if you come forward before being audited:

| Jurisdiction | Program | Penalty Reduction |
|-------------|---------|------------------|
| EU (most countries) | Voluntary correction | 50-75% reduction |
| UK | Unprompted disclosure to HMRC | Best available terms (typically 30% of max) |
| Australia | Voluntary disclosure | Significant reduction; no penalty if minor |
| US | Voluntary disclosure programs (VDP) | Varies by state; generally 50%+ reduction |

"Unprompted" means you contact the authority before they contact you. Once an audit is underway, you lose access to voluntary disclosure terms.

### Remediation Steps

1. Stop selling into the affected jurisdiction until registered (or switch to a MoR immediately)
2. Calculate total VAT owed retroactively from the date you crossed the threshold
3. File a voluntary disclosure with the relevant tax authority
4. Pay outstanding VAT + reduced penalty + accrued interest
5. Register going forward through the standard process

### Timelines

- Most authorities give **30-60 days** to respond to a penalty notice
- Appeal a final assessment within **30 days** of receiving it
- Voluntary disclosure responses typically processed in 30-90 days

### When to Get Professional Help

If total exposure exceeds **€5,000** or involves **multiple countries**, hire a VAT specialist. Typical cost for remediation: **€500-2,000** per engagement. This is almost always cheaper than the penalties you avoid.

## Digital Services Tax (DST) — NOT the Same as VAT

DST is a revenue-based tax on digital services. Unlike VAT (which is transaction-level) and corporate tax (which is profit-based), DST is calculated on gross revenue from digital services in a given market.

### Key Difference

VAT is collected from customers and remitted to the government. DST comes out of your own revenue — it is a cost, not a pass-through.

### DST Rates and Thresholds

| Country | DST Rate | Global Revenue Threshold | Local Revenue Threshold |
|---------|----------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| UK | 2% | £500M global | £25M UK |
| France | 3% | €750M global | €25M France |
| Italy | 3% | €750M global | €5.5M Italy |
| Spain | 3% | €750M global | €3M Spain |
| Turkey | 7.5% | €750M global | TRY 20M Turkey |
| India (Equalization Levy) | 2% | NO threshold | ₹2 crore (~$240K) |
| Kenya | 1.5% | NO threshold | KES 0 (all digital services) |

### Important for Solo Founders

The high global revenue thresholds in Europe (£500M, €750M) mean DST is **irrelevant** for virtually all solo founders and small teams.

**Exception — India and Kenya**: These two have no global revenue threshold:

- **India Equalization Levy**: Applies to non-resident e-commerce operators selling digital services or goods to Indian customers. Rate is 2% on gross consideration. Must register and file quarterly returns. Affects any Wyoming LLC, Singapore Pte., or other foreign entity selling SaaS or digital products to Indian B2C customers. Indian B2B customers may withhold this amount from payments — factor this into pricing.
- **Kenya Digital Service Tax**: 1.5% on gross transaction value. No minimum threshold. Applies to all foreign digital service providers with Kenyan customers.

### Practical Decision

| Your India/Kenya Revenue | Action |
|--------------------------|--------|
| < $10K/year | Monitor; enforcement is limited at this scale |
| $10K-$50K/year | Consult a tax advisor; weigh registration cost vs. exposure |
| > $50K/year | Register and comply; enforcement risk is real |

## Physical Goods & Cross-Border E-commerce

This KB focuses on digital services, but the rules differ significantly for physical goods. Key points for reference:

### US De Minimis (Section 321)

- Shipments valued at **$800 or less** per day per importer are exempt from customs duty and formal entry
- Above $800: customs duty applies, plus potential tariffs depending on product category and country of origin
- This threshold is under legislative review; check current rules before building a logistics model around it

### EU — De Minimis Abolished

| Rule | Pre-2021 | Post-2021 |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Import VAT threshold | €22 de minimis | No threshold — ALL imports subject to VAT |
| Customs duty | €150 threshold | €150 threshold still applies |
| Scheme for low-value goods | None | IOSS (Import One-Stop Shop) for goods <€150 |

**IOSS (Import One-Stop Shop)**: Lets non-EU sellers collect and remit VAT at point of sale for goods valued under €150. Without IOSS, the carrier or customs authority collects VAT from the buyer on delivery — a poor customer experience that increases cart abandonment.

### Key Differences from Digital Services

| Dimension | Digital Services | Physical Goods |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Delivery | Instant, borderless | Shipping, customs |
| VAT trigger | Customer location | Import entry |
| Additional taxes | DST (rare) | Customs duty, tariffs |
| Other compliance | Privacy, GDPR | Packaging regs, consumer protection |
| Complexity | Medium | High |

**Recommendation**: If selling physical goods to EU customers, either use the IOSS scheme or work with a fulfillment partner (e.g., ShipBob, Amazon FBA) who handles customs clearance and import VAT on your behalf.
