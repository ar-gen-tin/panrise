# Simple Architectures for One-Person Companies

## Architecture A: Single Entity (90% of Solo Founders)

### Structure
```
You (Individual)
  └── One Company (LLC / Ltd / Pte. Ltd. / OÜ)
        └── Receives all revenue, pays all expenses
```

### When to Use
- Revenue under $500K/year
- Customers primarily in one region
- You live in one country (or are nomadic)
- You want simplicity above all

→ See solo-structures.md decision tree

## Architecture B: Home Country + Foreign Company (Growing Solo Founder)

### Structure
```
You (Individual, tax resident in Country A)
  └── Foreign Company (in Country B, optimized for customers/tax)
        └── Pays you salary or distributions
```

### Examples
- **You in Germany + Estonia OÜ**: Retain profits at 0%, pay yourself a salary (taxed in Germany)
- **You in Thailand + Wyoming LLC**: LLC income passes through; Thailand doesn't tax foreign income (territorial)
- **You in Japan + Singapore Pte. Ltd.**: Singapore entity for Asia-Pacific clients, you draw salary taxed in Japan

### When to Use
- Revenue $50K-$500K
- Tax benefit of foreign entity outweighs compliance cost (~$2-5K/year extra)
- Your home country has high personal income tax (>30%)
- Your customers are primarily in a different region than where you live

### Key Rules
- You MUST pay yourself a reasonable salary (can't leave everything in the company forever)
- CFC rules may require you to report/pay tax on undistributed profits
- Your company needs "substance" in its jurisdiction (management activities, not just a mailbox)

## Architecture C: Nomad + Zero-Tax Entity (Tax-Optimized Nomad)

### Structure
```
You (tax resident in territorial/zero-tax country)
  └── Company (in customer-friendly jurisdiction)
        └── All profits flow through; minimal/zero personal tax
```

### Examples
- **You in Dubai + Dubai Freezone LLC**: 9% corporate + 0% personal = ~5-8% effective
- **You in Panama + Wyoming LLC**: 0% US tax (non-resident) + 0% Panama tax (foreign income) = ~0%
- **You in Georgia + Estonia OÜ**: 1% micro-business rate in Georgia if revenue <GEL 500K

### When to Use
- You're willing to physically relocate
- Revenue >$100K (to justify relocation logistics)
- You have no US citizenship (US citizens can't escape US tax this way)
- You can establish genuine residency (not just a passport stamp)

### Critical Requirements
- Actually LIVE in the zero/low-tax country (not just visit once a year)
- Get a local tax residency certificate
- Close ties to previous high-tax country (or they'll claim you're still resident)
- Maintain company substance in jurisdiction of incorporation

## When You DON'T Need a Company

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Testing an idea, no revenue yet | Just start. Invoice personally. |
| Revenue < $5K/year | Sole proprietorship in your home country |
| All clients in your home country | Local sole proprietorship or auto-entrepreneur |
| US citizen, simple freelancing | US LLC in your state of residence |
| Receiving payments from 1-2 platforms | Platform handles tax withholding |

## When to Consider TWO Entities

Most solo founders never need more than one entity. Consider two only if:
- Revenue > $500K AND split between very different regions (US + Asia)
- You need a US entity for Stripe/credibility AND a local entity for tax residency
- You're transitioning from one country to another (temporary overlap)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Over-engineering**: Setting up a Singapore + Delaware + Estonia structure at $30K revenue
2. **Ignoring home country tax**: Your LLC in Wyoming doesn't exempt you from paying tax where you live
3. **No substance**: Registering in Singapore with no director, no office, no presence
4. **Forgetting US self-employment tax**: US citizens owe ~15.3% SE tax even abroad
5. **CFC trap**: US citizen opens foreign corp → triggers expensive Form 5471 + GILTI
6. **Double structures without need**: Paying $5K+/year to maintain two entities when one suffices
