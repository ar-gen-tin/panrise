# Intellectual Property & Trademark Protection for Solo Founders

## What IP Do Solo Founders Have?

| IP Type | What It Covers | Protection | Cost |
|---------|---------------|------------|------|
| **Trademark** | Brand name, logo, tagline | Registration required | $250-2,000+ |
| **Copyright** | Code, content, designs, courses | Automatic (no registration needed) | $0 |
| **Trade secret** | Algorithms, customer lists, processes | Keep it secret | $0 |
| **Patent** | Inventions, unique processes | Registration required | $5,000-15,000+ |

**For most solo founders**: Copyright is automatic, trademarks matter, patents rarely apply, trade secrets are free.

## Trademark Protection

### When to Register a Trademark
- You have a brand name you plan to use long-term
- You sell products/services under that name
- Revenue >$10K/year (below this, the cost may not justify)
- Someone could confuse their brand with yours (or vice versa)

### Where to Register

#### Home Country First
Register in the country where you do the most business or where your company is incorporated.

| Country | Office | Cost | Timeline |
|---------|--------|------|----------|
| US | USPTO | $250-350 per class | 8-12 months |
| EU (all 27 countries) | EUIPO | €850 first class | 4-6 months |
| UK | UKIPO | £170 first class | 4-6 months |
| China | CNIPA | ¥270 (~$40) per class | 9-12 months |
| Japan | JPO | ¥12,000 (~$80) per class | 6-12 months |
| Singapore | IPOS | SGD 341 per class | 6-9 months |
| Hong Kong | HKIPD | HKD 2,000 per class | 6-9 months |

#### Going International: Madrid Protocol
- File ONE application through your home country's IP office
- Designate countries where you want protection (100+ member countries)
- Cheaper and simpler than filing separately in each country
- Cost: ~CHF 653 base + CHF 100-300 per designated country
- Requires existing home country registration or application first

#### China: File Early, File First
- China uses **first-to-file** system (not first-to-use like US)
- Trademark squatters register foreign brands before the brand enters China
- **If you have ANY plans to serve Chinese market**: File in China EARLY
- Cost is very low (¥270 per class) — no reason to delay

### Trademark Classes (Nice Classification)

You must specify which "classes" your trademark covers. Common ones for solo founders:

| Class | Covers | Typical Solo Founder Use |
|-------|--------|------------------------|
| 9 | Software, apps, SaaS | Any digital product |
| 35 | Advertising, business management, consulting | Consulting, agencies |
| 41 | Education, training, courses | Course creators, coaches |
| 42 | Software development, IT services | Dev agencies, SaaS |
| 25 | Clothing | Merch, fashion brands |
| 38 | Telecommunications | Chat apps, communication platforms |

**Tip**: Each additional class costs more. Start with 1-2 classes most relevant to your business.

### DIY vs Lawyer

| Approach | Cost | Best When |
|----------|------|-----------|
| DIY (file yourself) | $250-850 | Simple word mark, one country, clear class |
| Online service (Trademarkia, LegalZoom) | $500-1,500 | Word mark, want someone to check conflicts |
| IP lawyer | $1,500-5,000 | Complex mark, multiple countries, potential conflicts |

**For most solo founders**: DIY for US (TEAS Plus on USPTO.gov) or EU (EUIPO online). It's straightforward for simple word marks.

## Copyright (Automatic Protection)

### What's Protected Automatically
- Source code you write
- Blog posts, articles, course content
- Designs, illustrations, UI mockups
- Videos, podcasts, music you create
- Documentation

### What's NOT Protected
- Ideas, concepts, methods
- Facts and data
- Names and short phrases (those are trademarks)
- Functional aspects of software (those might be patents)

### Key Actions for Solo Founders

1. **Contractor IP assignment**: ALWAYS include IP assignment clause in contractor agreements. Without it, the contractor may own the copyright to code/designs they create for you.

2. **Open source awareness**: If you use open source libraries, understand their licenses:
   - **MIT/BSD/Apache**: Very permissive, use freely in commercial products
   - **GPL**: Copyleft — if you modify and distribute, you must open-source your modifications
   - **AGPL**: Network copyleft — even SaaS usage triggers open-source obligation
   - **No license**: All rights reserved by the author — you technically can't use it

3. **AI-generated content**: Copyright status is evolving. US Copyright Office currently does not grant copyright to purely AI-generated works. If AI assists but human makes creative choices, copyright likely applies. Keep records of your creative input.

## Domain Name Strategy

### Priority Domains to Secure
1. `.com` — Primary (non-negotiable for global business)
2. Country-specific TLDs for your key markets (`.co.uk`, `.jp`, `.de`)
3. Common misspellings of your brand name (if affordable)

### Protecting Your Domain
- Enable domain privacy (WHOIS protection)
- Set auto-renewal (losing a domain is painful)
- Use a registrar with strong security (Cloudflare, Namecheap)
- Enable registrar lock to prevent unauthorized transfers

### Domain Disputes (UDRP)
- If someone squats on your brand name as a domain, you can file a UDRP complaint
- Cost: ~$1,500 for a single-panelist decision
- Requires: You have trademark rights, the domain was registered in bad faith
- Timeline: 2-3 months

## Trade Secrets for Solo Founders

Things that give you competitive advantage and should stay confidential:
- Customer lists and pricing data
- Proprietary algorithms or processes
- Business strategies and financial projections
- Supplier relationships and terms

### Protection Steps
- Use NDAs with contractors and partners
- Mark confidential documents as "CONFIDENTIAL"
- Limit access on a need-to-know basis
- Use proper access controls on code repos and documents

## When to Get IP Help

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Simple word mark, one country | DIY filing |
| Someone is copying your brand | Consult IP lawyer ($200-500 for initial assessment) |
| You're entering China market | File trademark in China immediately (can DIY) |
| Licensing your IP to others | IP lawyer for license agreement ($1,000-3,000) |
| Potential patent | Patent attorney (expensive, $5K+; evaluate if truly needed) |
| Received a cease & desist | IP lawyer immediately |
