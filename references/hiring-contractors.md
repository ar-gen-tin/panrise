# Hiring & Paying International Contractors for Solo Founders

## The Core Question: Contractor vs Employee?

Most solo founders hire **contractors** (not employees). This is correct and preferred — but misclassification is a serious legal risk.

### Contractor vs Employee Test (Universal Principles)

| Factor | Contractor | Employee |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Control over HOW work is done | No (you control WHAT, they control HOW) | Yes |
| Set schedule/hours | No | Yes |
| Provide tools/equipment | No (they use their own) | Yes |
| Exclusive relationship | No (they can work for others) | Yes (typically) |
| Duration | Project-based or defined term | Ongoing/indefinite |
| Integration into your business | Peripheral | Core function |

**Rule of thumb**: If they use their own laptop, set their own hours, work for multiple clients, and deliver a defined output → contractor. If they sit in your "team" full-time doing ongoing work with your tools → likely employee.

### Risk of Misclassification

- **US**: IRS reclassification → back taxes + penalties (Form SS-8 audit)
- **EU**: Fines up to €50K+ per worker; must pay social contributions retroactively
- **India**: Provident Fund + gratuity liability retroactively
- **Australia**: Superannuation (11.5%) + penalties
- **General**: If a country decides your contractor is an employee, YOU owe their benefits, tax withholding, and social contributions — potentially years of back pay

### Required Documentation

**From every contractor, collect BEFORE first payment:**
- Signed contractor agreement (scope, rate, IP assignment, termination)
- Invoice for each payment (their legal name, address, amount, description)
- W-8BEN (if your company is US entity and contractor is non-US)
- W-9 (if contractor is US person and your company is US entity)
- Tax ID or equivalent in their country

**You must issue annually (US entities):**
- 1099-NEC for US contractors paid >$600
- No 1099 needed for non-US contractors (W-8BEN on file suffices)

## Employer of Record (EOR) Services

When you need to hire someone as an **employee** in another country (required by local law or preference), use an EOR. The EOR is the legal employer; you manage the work.

### When You NEED an EOR

- Contractor works exclusively for you, full-time, for >6 months
- Local law in their country makes contractor relationship illegal for this type of work
- You want to offer benefits (health insurance, equity) to attract talent
- The person is in a high-risk country for misclassification (France, Spain, Netherlands, Brazil)

### Do You Actually Need an EOR?

For most solo founders: **NO**. You need an EOR only when:
- Revenue >$100K AND you have long-term "team members" (not project-based help)
- You're hiring in strict labor law countries (France, Brazil, India for FTEs)
- You want to offer equity compensation to key people

**For 90% of solo founders**: Pay contractors via Wise Business with a proper agreement. That's it.

## Contractor Agreement Essentials

Every contractor agreement should include:

1. **Scope of work** — What they'll deliver, NOT how they'll do it
2. **Payment terms** — Rate (hourly/project/monthly retainer), currency, payment schedule, method
3. **IP assignment** — All work product belongs to your company (critical for code, design, content)
4. **Confidentiality** — NDA clause protecting your business information
5. **Termination** — How either party can end the relationship (typically 14-30 days notice)
6. **Independent contractor status** — Explicit statement that this is NOT employment
7. **Indemnification** — Contractor responsible for their own taxes and compliance
8. **Governing law** — Which jurisdiction's law applies to disputes

### Country-Specific Gotchas

| Country | Watch Out For |
|---------|--------------|
| **India** | GST registration required if contractor earns >₹20L/year; TDS (tax deducted at source) may apply |
| **Philippines** | Contractors must register as self-employed with BIR; you may need to withhold 2% EWT |
| **Brazil** | Extremely strict on misclassification; any hint of employment relationship = full labor rights |
| **France** | "Auto-entrepreneur" status exists but courts frequently reclassify to employee |
| **Germany** | "Scheinselbständigkeit" (fake self-employment) if >5/6 of income from one client |
| **UK** | IR35 rules: if contractor works like an employee, YOU pay employer NI contributions |
| **Japan** | Distinction is strict; regular/exclusive contractors often reclassified |

## Tax Implications of Paying Contractors

### For Your Company
- Contractor payments are tax-deductible business expenses
- No payroll tax, social security, or benefits cost
- Must issue 1099-NEC (US) for US contractors >$600/year

### For the Contractor
- They handle their own income tax and social contributions
- In their country, they're typically a sole proprietor or have their own company
- YOUR obligation: collect W-8BEN (non-US) or W-9 (US) and pay via documented invoice

### Transfer Pricing (When It Matters)
- If you pay a contractor who is also a related party (spouse, family member, your own foreign entity), the payment must be at "arm's length" (market rate)
- Tax authorities scrutinize related-party payments as potential profit shifting
- For solo founders: this mainly matters if you have a holding company paying your operating company or vice versa
