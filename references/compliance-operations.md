# Compliance Operations & Annual Calendar for Solo Founders

## 1. Annual Compliance Calendar by Jurisdiction

| Jurisdiction | Filing | Deadline | Penalty for Late |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wyoming LLC | Annual Report | 1st day of anniversary month | $50 late fee, then $2/month |
| Wyoming LLC | Registered Agent renewal | Annual | LLC dissolved if unpaid |
| Delaware LLC | Franchise Tax | June 1 | $200 penalty + 1.5%/month interest |
| Delaware LLC | Annual Report | March 1 | $200 penalty |
| US (any LLC, non-US owner) | Form 5472 + pro-forma 1120 | April 15 (or extension) | $25,000 per form per year |
| US (any LLC, US owner) | Schedule C / 1065 | April 15 | Varies |
| Singapore | Annual Return (ACRA) | Within 30 days of AGM | SGD 300 late fee |
| Singapore | Corporate Tax (Form C-S) | November 30 | Penalties + estimated assessment |
| Estonia OÜ | Annual Report (e-Business Register) | Within 6 months of fiscal year end | €100-3,200 fine |
| UK Ltd | Confirmation Statement | Anniversary of incorporation | £250 late fee, then striking off |
| UK Ltd | Accounts filing | 9 months after FY end | £150-1,500 penalty |
| Hong Kong | Annual Return (NAR1) | Within 42 days of anniversary | HKD 870-3,480 late fee |
| Hong Kong | Profits Tax Return | Within 1 month of issuance | Penalty + estimated assessment |
| Dubai Freezone | License renewal | Annual | Varies by freezone; license cancellation |

**CRITICAL: US Form 5472** — every single-member LLC owned by a non-US person MUST file this. $25,000 penalty per form per year if missed. Most common mistake for non-US founders with Wyoming/Delaware LLC.

## 3. Audit Preparation Checklist

What to keep ready at all times:

- Bank statements (all accounts, all months)
- Invoices issued (every single one, with customer details)
- Invoices received (for expenses claimed as deductions)
- Contracts with clients and contractors
- Proof of where you physically worked (for PE defense): flight records, co-working receipts, Airbnb bookings
- Board meeting minutes (even informal ones, for substance defense)
- Tax residency certificate from your country of residence
- W-8BEN / W-9 forms from contractors
- Operating Agreement for LLC
- Retention period: keep everything for 7 years (most jurisdictions require 5-7)

## 5. Currency Hedging for Solo Founders

Simple strategies that actually work:

- Hold revenue in the currency you'll spend it in (natural hedge)
- Use Wise's multi-currency account to hold USD/EUR/GBP separately
- Convert in batches (monthly/quarterly) rather than all at once
- Wise rate alerts: set a target rate and convert automatically when hit
- For larger amounts ($50K+): consider forward contracts (available via Wise Business, OFX, or banks)
- Don't try to "time" the market — dollar-cost averaging is the simplest strategy
